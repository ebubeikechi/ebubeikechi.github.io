Childhood & Creativity

Curiosity Started Before Technology

Long before spreadsheets, automation, or systems design, music became one of the earliest things that shaped how I think. Playing the piano trained my ear for patterns, timing, structure, repetition, and flow. What started as passion slowly became a way of understanding how different moving parts can work together harmoniously.

Growing up also came with uncertainty, limitations, and moments of self-doubt, but those experiences built resilience early. I became naturally drawn to solving problems, observing inefficiencies, and finding smarter ways to approach everyday challenges.

Education & Analytical Thinking

Mathematics Changed How I See Systems

Studying Mathematics and Statistics strengthened my ability to think logically, break down complexity, and work through problems systematically. It trained me to pay attention to structure, precision, patterns, and relationships hidden inside data and processes.

Beyond academics, it shaped my mindset. I became increasingly interested in how systems behave under pressure, how inefficiencies compound over time, and how small process improvements can create massive operational impact.

Finance, Operations & Automation

Process Automation Became My Direction

Working in finance, settlement, and reconciliation operations exposed me to the hidden reality behind large-scale transaction systems, repetitive workflows, operational bottlenecks, reporting pressure, manual errors, and constant reconciliation demands.

Instead of accepting inefficiency as normal, I became deeply interested in improving processes through automation. I started building solutions using Excel VBA, Google Sheets automation, SQL, and console-based tools to reduce repetition, improve accuracy, and simplify operational workflows.

Today, my work sits at the intersection of operations, analytical thinking, and automation, designing systems that help people work smarter, faster, and with greater clarity.
